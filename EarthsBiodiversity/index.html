Biodiversity, according to the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) is the variability among living organisms such as animals and plants from all sources including terrestrial and aquatic environment on earth of which they are part. It is primarily in-connection with conservation and protection of nature. Thus, it plays an important role in maintaining the stability of our ecosystem, regulating climate condition and providing raw materials for food or industrial products.
The continuous expansion of human activities have led to the habitat loss of many animals and plants. Major causes of the said problem are Deforestation and Illegal hunting of animals in the wild. Deforestation creates massive cutting/clearing of plants particularly trees in the forest and it decreases basically the availability of earth’s land for millions of species wherein 70 percent of this area is occupied by animals and plants (Depre, 2014). On the other hand, Illegal hunting of animals contributes huge impact by which there is species extinction. Killing those animals in the wild without permission of the government or for the purpose of illegal trading is widely happening around the world and it directly affects the population and distribution of animals in the ecosystem. Overall, habitat loss and species extinction are the effects of these human activities which basically results into unstable earth’s biodiversity.
Nevertheless, various organizations around the world are continuously advocating the conservation and protection of ecosystem towards a sustainable use of animals and plant diversity on earth.  One of which is the International Union for Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources (IUCN) that aims for monitoring the current situation of species in the world as well as providing awareness to the people through their programs, forums and events that primarily  concerned on sustainability of our natural resources. Based on their monitoring report, there are estimated 30 million species on earth and only 1.75 million species of these are known so far (2012). Unfortunately, 60% plants, 20% vertebrates and 30% of all organisms are in danger of going extinct. This is an implication that nature must be considered one of the main agenda not only in the government or organizations around the world but also in the community.
This is a challenge now that even a student have the power to make a change through awareness campaign that is focus on the current situation of animals, plants and its habitats. Students can do this, it just needs the willingness of them to act and to learn. Learning prior to action is an essential step of making a big change particularly in the community. A Change maker must have enough information about this issue of decreasing population of species, habitat loss and other biodiversity matter.
This activity provides an updated information related to biodiversity, such as Species diversity, Hierarchical taxonomic system of classification, and Protection and conservation of endangered and economically important species. These (3) topics are enough to give the necessary ideas and background knowledge about diversity of plants and animals in the ecosystem and allows every individual to explain the concept of a species, to classify organisms using the hierarchical taxonomic system and so explaining the advantage of high biodiversity in maintaining the stability of an ecosystem.
